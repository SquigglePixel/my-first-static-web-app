Adventures

 

Packrafting

The Gila Region is home to a high density of perennial streams, as compared to most other areas within the arid Southwest, making packrafting the perfect mode of transportation to explore this remote and scenic wilderness destination. GAILS is proud to offer beginner – intermediate instruction, guided overnight clinics and equipment rentals to support your paddling adventures!

Llama Trekking

The GAILS rancho has been home to three “Classic” pack llamas for over a decade now. These sure-footed camelids are fully trained for panier-style packing and can carry 75-100 lbs. of gear per animal. Visitors and campers can hang out, photograph and hand feed them or enroll in ½ - full day clinic on handling and packing techniques.

Traditional Fly or Tenkara Fishing

Whether you are interested in learning technique or just getting out to bag the elusive native Gila Trout, GAILS is ideally situated to explore the proximate waters, with the heart of Gila trout habitat being the Gila and Aldo Leopold Wilderness Areas. The once endangered species are beautiful, resilient, and a pleasure to angle for, as much a part of the region's character as the Gila River itself.

Disc (Frisbee) Golf

The GAILS property boasts a private 15-hole disc golf course that plays across the San Fransisco River and spans the entire 6-acre rancho property. While the course lacks official driving pads and signage, every “hole” utilized PDGA approved chain-style baskets. The evolving layout is truly one of the more unique and scenic disc golf courses you will ever play! Cost summary = $5 greens fee or free with other services (donations for maintenance always welcome).

 

Clinics

 

Packraft 101

Basic ½ or single day clinic that covers everything packrafting, including: the basics of equipment (boat, pumps, paddle, PFD, helmet, drysuit, dry bags, etc.), paddling skills and on-water dynamics. Costs summary: $99 for land-based portion ½ day clinic or $189 for full day clinic, including 2 hours of on-water instruction. Limited boats and equipment available for rent via GAILS LLC.

Packraft 101 Lab

A full day review encompassing on-water drills and paddling, where you can practice and put your newly acquired skills to the test. Skills focus: basic paddle strokes, boat control, peel-out and eddy turns, reading whitewater, day packing considerations, recognizing river features and hazards and basic rescue equipment use and techniques. Costs summary: $189 stand-alone clinic or $349 discount combo clinic when combined with Packraft 101.

Gila Primer

2-day overnight clinic for Class II aptitude paddlers - focused on unique regional aspects and hazards, such as: remoteness, narrow canyon dynamics, wood and anthropocentric hazards, multi-day considerations (gear packing, logistics, tec.), LNT ethics, land ownership, etc. Learn concepts and perspectives you won’t elsewhere and align expectation for Gila Region multi-day trips. Clinic is generally held on the middle San Franciso River or Coming in 2024 AZ Gila Box RNCA – weather/flows permitting. Cost summary: $378/person.

Deluxe Packraft Package

Includes registration for some combination of the Packraft 101, Packraft 101L or Gila Primer clinics -depending on participant ability. Designed for those who plan to invest some time in the region and desire or more cohesive clinic spanning 3-4 days. Cost summary $500/person or check for group discount.

Essential Packrafting Eligibility Criteria

Courses and trips are open to all individuals who acknowledge the ability to perform the following Essential Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Breathe independently (i.e., not require medical devices to sustain breathing)
  2. Independently maintain sealed airway passages while under water
  3. Independently hold head upright without neck / head support
  4. Manage personal care independently or with assistance of a companion
  5. Manage personal mobility independently or with a reasonable amount of assistance
  6. Follow instructions and effectively communicate independently or with assistance of a companion
  7. Independently turn from face-down to face-up and remain floating face up while wearing a properly fitted personal flotation device (PFD)
  8. Independently swim to or from the shore and away from river hazards while wearing a properly fitted personal flotation device (PFD)
  9. Get on or off, or in and out, of a paddle-craft independently or with a reasonable amount of assistance
  10. Independently get out and from under a capsized paddle-craft
  11. Remount or reenter the paddle-craft following deep water capsize independently or with a reasonable amount of assistance
  12. Independently enter and exit the water in a lake, river or stream
  13. Maintain a safe body position while attempting skills, activities and rescues listed in the appropriate course description(s), and have the ability to recognize and identify to others when such efforts would be unsafe given your personal situation

These criteria represent the minimum standards necessary to maintain the safety of participants and instructors, as well as the overall course experience. Inability to meet these criteria will disqualify a person from participating in a course with GAILS. None of these criteria are meant to discriminate on the basis of any physical or mental disability. They are applied uniformly to all potential trip participants, irrespective of the presence or absence of any disability. If you have any questions about whether or not you are eligible to participate, please do not hesitate to contact us.

Llama Handling and Trekking

Basic ½ to full day clinics spotlighting all things llama, including: biology, domestication history, feeding, handling, stringing, packing equipment and transport considerations. Full day, includes a short trek where you get to lead a llama on the trail. Satisfactory completion of full day clinic is required for potential llama rental. Cost summary: $49/person = ½-day clinic and $99/person = full day trek clinic. Ask about group/family discounts.
